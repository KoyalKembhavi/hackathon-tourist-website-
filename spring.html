Japan's Must-Visit Spring Destinations

"Step into a sea of blossoms — discover the magic of Japan in full bloom this spring!"

Kyoto

Kyoto blends cultural heritage with the beauty of spring in the most elegant way. From the Philosopher’s Path to Kiyomizu-dera Temple, cherry blossoms envelop the city in pink hues. Visitors enjoy tea ceremonies, geisha sightings in Gion, and peaceful walks through bamboo groves under blooming trees. Kyoto is the perfect place to experience Japan’s past and nature in harmony.

Ueno Park

Ueno Park in Tokyo is one of the country's most famous cherry blossom viewing spots. Every spring, over a thousand cherry trees line the park’s central path, creating a tunnel of pink petals. The lively atmosphere, complete with food stalls and traditional hanami (flower viewing) parties, makes it a top spot for experiencing Japan’s spring culture in the heart of the capital.

Mount Yoshino

Mount Yoshino in Nara is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Japan’s oldest and most celebrated cherry blossom destinations. With over 30,000 sakura trees planted along its slopes, the mountain offers a breathtaking layered view of blossoms from different elevations. Visitors can hike up the trails for stunning panoramic views and spiritual encounters at ancient temples like Kinpusen-ji.
