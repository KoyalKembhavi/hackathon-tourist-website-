Japan's Must-Visit Winter Destinations

"Let Japan Warm Your Soul This Winter – One Snowflake at a Time."

Jigokudani Monkey Park

Located in Nagano Prefecture, Jigokudani Monkey Park is famous for its Japanese macaques (snow monkeys) who soak in natural hot springs during winter. Surrounded by snowy landscapes, this unique spectacle offers a close-up view of wildlife in a serene, natural setting.

Snow Festival, Sapporo

Held every February in Hokkaido’s capital, the Sapporo Snow Festival showcases massive ice and snow sculptures, illuminated streets, and winter fun for all ages. Odori Park, Susukino, and Tsudome sites feature everything from towering ice castles to local food stalls and snow slides.

Hokkaido and Nagano

Home to the 1998 Winter Olympics, Nagano offers world-class skiing and snowboarding in resorts like Hakuba and Nozawa Onsen. With powdery snow, scenic mountain views, and charming traditional villages, it’s a top destination for winter sports enthusiasts and first-timers alike.
